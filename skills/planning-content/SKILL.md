# Content Strategy Planning

Plan content calendars and topic selection that align with business goals. This skill focuses on what to publish and when -- not on writing the content itself.

## When to Use

- Planning a weekly, monthly, or quarterly content calendar
- Deciding which topics or themes to prioritize
- Allocating effort across platforms (X, LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram)
- Aligning content output with a product launch, campaign, or growth goal

## Process

1. Clarify the time horizon (week, month, quarter) and business objective (audience growth, lead gen, brand awareness, product launch).
2. Identify active platforms and their posting cadence.
3. Allocate content across pillars: Founder's Journey (40%), Behind-the-Scenes Ops (25%), Case Studies & Proof (20%), Industry Hot Takes (15%).
4. Map a default weekly rhythm: Monday (recap), Tuesday (behind-the-scenes), Wednesday (case study), Thursday (hot take), Friday (serialized update).
5. Plan repurposing -- one strong piece becomes 3-4 platform-native posts.
6. Leave 20% capacity for reactive/trending content.
7. Set measurable targets per platform and timeframe.

## Output Format

- **Weekly plan**: Table with Day / Platform / Pillar / Topic / Format / Notes.
- **Monthly calendar**: Week-by-week themes, key pieces, production schedule, metric targets.
- **Quarterly strategy**: Narrative arc, platform evolution, pillar adjustments, resource needs.

## Constraints

- Never plan content that requires fabricating experiences.
- Quality over volume -- fewer posts with impact beats daily filler.
- The calendar is a guide, not a mandate. Skip anything that is not genuinely interesting.
- Always account for available production capacity before committing to volume.
